--- layout: post title: '' categories: - Whatever tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: {} --- Reading Layard's Happiness, I've become overwhelmed by the insulting moral reductionism of his vulgar Benthamism. It's becoming increasingly clear to me that technocratic welfare liberalism cannot survive the demise of analytical monism. If preference satisfaction is not the only thing that matters normatively, or if happiness is not the only thing that matters normatively, and we are left with an "objective list" of potentially incommensurable values, then there can be no justification for technocratic or paternalistic command. There is no set of facts about what makes individual lives valuable, or worth living, that a special class of people can be in a priveleged position to appreciate
