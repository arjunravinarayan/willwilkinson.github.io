--- layout: post title: Vacation categories: [] tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: {} --- I've found it amusing that some people are semi-scandalized by the fact that it is not illegal in the United States to offer a labor contract with no paid vacation. You'd think this lack of coercion in American labor markets would be something to be proud of. People complaining about the relative freedom of American workers to negotiate the terms of their own labor agreements need to absorb one of the lessons of Glen Whitman's essay on unintended consequences: the multiplicity of margins of choice. If I've bargain up my paid vacation, then I've probably bargained down my pay.
