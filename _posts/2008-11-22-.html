[B]eyond the tautological, what does this demand that conservatives recognize that Obama is their president mean?   This is a nit I love to pick, so I am going to pick it. We are not subjects of a sovereign. We are democratic citizens, with legal rights. And before we are citizens, we are persons. And persons have moral rights, simply in virtue of being persons, and independent of citizenship. George W. Bush is not my president. He doesn't preside over me. He is President of the United States, the head of state and head of government of the organization, the United States of America, which issued my passport and has some jurisdiction in the physical region in which I reside. He is the head of the executive branch of the United States government with certain powers enumerated in the Constitution. If George W. Bush comes to my door, I don't have to let him in. If he tells me to jump, I can tell him to screw himself. He is commander in chief of the U.S. armed forces, but he is no sense whatsoever commander of me. It is the duty of the the federal executive to enforce federal law, and I am subject to federal law. But I am more often and more immediately affected by local law, and I have a duty to obey only insofar as the law is legitimate, and at times I have a duty to disobey. We are free and equal people.
