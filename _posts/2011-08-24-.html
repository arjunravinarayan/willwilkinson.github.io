Thus Hayek (1933b, pp. 164-65) held that an increased public demand to hold (i.e. to "hoard" or not spend) deposit balances would have undesirable deflationary consequences unless offset by deposit expansion:

Unless the banks create additional credits for investment purposesto the same extent that the holders of deposits have ceased to usethem for current expenditure, the effect of such saving isessentially the same as that of hoarding [of base money] and has all the undesirable deflationary consequences attaching to the latter.
